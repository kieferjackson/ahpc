About Us

Advocate Hospice was started to promote excellent patient care at the end of life by Dr. Kevin Jackson and his spouse Barbara Jackson, who strive to provide outstanding and innovative patient care delivery.
Dr. and Mrs. Jackson have previously owned and operated multiple medical practices across Maricopa County since 2001, all sharing the goal of improving patient care in the Valley. Advocate Hospice is their latest creation with the philosophy of providing excellent palliative care, sustaining the highest possible quality of life until death, and using their experience and expertise to deliver unrivaled hospice care.


Advocates for your comfort

We believe that life should conclude naturally with a focus on quality of life rather than the length of life. Also important to maintaining physical wellbeing is emotional and spiritual support, which we also offer to patients and their families.
Our team of professionals offer support to families and their loved ones, with the core belief being that each one of us has the right to die pain free and with dignity. Advocate Hospice promises to provide all of the support necessary to families' loved ones to accomplish this goal.


Our Mission

Advocate Hospice will:

Provide excellent palliative care to individuals and their families with life limiting illnesses that have chosen to seek comfort care and symptom management.
Treat comprehensively including physical, psychosocial, and spiritual comfort while caring for an individual and their families.
Neither hasten nor postpone death
Support individuals and their families through the process of death and bereavement.


Services

We provide the following services throughout the majority of Maricopa county:

Physician Services
Meeting with patients and their families in order to assess whether or not hospice is best for the patient, in addition to diagnosing and treating symptoms.
Nursing Care
Routine visits with the patient to treat symptoms and administer medical care.
Medical Equipment
Related to terminal illness, which may include: hospital bed, specialty mattress, wheelchair, walker, oxygen.
Drugs for Symptom Control or Pain Relief
All medications related to terminal illness for pain and symptom management prescribed by the patient's physician.
Hospice Aide Services
Housekeeping and assistance with hygiene duties: bathing, dressing, hair and oral care, feeding, and ensuring patients' comfort.
Physical Therapy
Treating physical disabilities that restrict a patient's mobility using hands-on care and exercise to restore body movement.
Occupational Therapy
Treating a patient's physical and/or cognitive problems with daily activities chosen by the patient and designed to engage their interest.
Speech-language Pathology Services
Therapy aimed to treat patients with difficulty swallowing in addition to treating communication problems and speech disorders.
Social Worker Services
Helping patients and their families to understand and plan for end-of-life care, including access to support networks for overcoming emotional, familial, and financial struggles.
Chaplain Services
Offering spiritual guidance for patients and their families at the end-of-life in addition to accommodating religious practices for patients when requested.
Dietary Counseling
Guides patients to form healthy eating habits in addition to formulating diet plans with nutritious foods.
Grief and Loss Counseling
Offers patients and their families bereavement support for grief and loss for up to 13 months after a patient's death.
Volunteer Services
Providing patients with companionship through readings, playing games, performing errands, and offering support to patients and their families.
